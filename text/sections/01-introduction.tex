\begin{enumerate}
    \item Refine the randomisation argument for Ed PGI.
    Using only the one copy gene variants from each parent, and comparing the results to previous research (i.e., Carvhalo 2024).
    \item Structural model for the mediation analysis of Ed PGI, using the Roy model and control function identification to attain identification.    
\end{enumerate}

Section map for this paper:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item Intro (done last).
    \item Data + biological context.
    \item Section on the adjusted PGI randomisation design, clearly stating estimands
    \item New results of genetic effects.
    \item Conceptual framework for mediation effects, clearly stating estimands
    \item Results of the mediation analysis.
    \item Conclusion.
\end{enumerate}
Note: a major concern for the Ed PGI previous papers is that they have little economic usage.
This paper can be framed as changing that, investigating the economic system underlying the Ed PGI causal effects, giving economic credence where before there was merely concern.

This paper proceeds as follows.
\autoref{sec:data} summarises the UKB data, and explains how the Ed PGI is constructed.
\autoref{sec:genetics} shows a causal research design, based on Mendelian independent in heritance, for estimating the causal effects of the Ed PGI; 
\autoref{sec:genetic-results}

shows estimates of the causal effects of the Ed PGI, on education and labour market outcomes.
\autoref{sec:directindirect} gives a conceptual framework for estimating the direct and indirect effects of genetics and education, in this observational setting;
\autoref{sec:results} gives the empirical results.
%\autoref{sec:discussion} discusses the approach, and its applicability to other topics;
\autoref{sec:discussion} concludes.
