\subsection{Random Variation in Genetic Inheritance}
\label{sec:genetics}
Genes are not randomly assigned to children.
Genes are inherited from parents, where children receive 13 chromosomes from each parent at conception --- half the genes from their mother, half from father.

Parents' traits, such their socioeconomic status, may differ for parents with higher or lower PGI values, confounding association-based estimates of PGI genetic effects among their children.

\subsection{Addressing Measurement Error in the PGI}

Mention that PGI weights are calculated with statistical error, so estimates of their effects are attentuated in un-normalised regressions.
This can be ameliorated using two PGIs, with (supposedly) uncorrelated measurement errors, to instrument for eachother in the obviously related framework.
